Hour of Code

Code.org has developed an international movement with its “Hour of Code” to transform coding from an intimidating feeling for everybody into an interactive exercise. The initiative de-mythifies coding in promoting a participating and digitally literate society: Everyone can learn the basics via guided tutorials, often through block-based programming.

When & Where?

- On November 4th, Aysu, Kuzey, Ferid, and Nicat attended a Hour of Code event at Kaspi Education Center in Khatai: for teaching basic concepts of coding, introduction to code.org and providing information about ADA University.

What about the organization of the event, what activities were included?

- The audience was chosen on the basis of needs and compatibility. In this event, participants were first greeted and introduced to ADA University, technology, and computer science-related academic programs and career opportunities. During the main activity.code.org was introduced by Nicat through features and simple coding exercises. Ferid and Aysu provided one-on-one personal assistance and coaching to help students move on with confidence. Photos and videos of the event taken by Kuzey show enthusiasm and interest in coding. Many were surprised by the ease of learning and gained a new perspective on computer science possibilities.

Have we faced with any issues?

- The Hour of Code event went on pretty smoothly and was relatively stress-free. Minor problems in the internet connection were sorted out quickly by using mobile hotspots. This preparedness and time management helped catch up with schedules and build confidence for similar events, emphasizing the importance of backups. Participants felt confident and became curious after going through the introductory activities on code.org. They were interested in learning more and wanted to continue. The team spirit contributed to the positive flow of activities, though there is room for improvement for more time management and initial troubleshooting sessions. The Hour of Code turned out to be a very enriching experience, both for the participants and educators in the basics of coding, problem-solving, and computer science. It was teamwork-lead, supported by Kaspi Education Center, eager participants, supportive group members, and ADA University. The event had left the participants with a sense of achievement and eagerness to learn more.
